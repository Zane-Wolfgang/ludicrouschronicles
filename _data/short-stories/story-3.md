“Surely you jest?” Zane asked, arms behind his back as he inclined his head to the mistress, whom of which practically radiated joy as if she were a condensed second sun.

“It’s a place most delightful.” She’d respond, gliding back to Zane and linking their arms, causing him to jolt with a ripple of terror cascading down his spine (or in reality, embarrassment, but he’d never allow himself to admit that for even a moment).

“In hindsight, reservations should have been made prior.” She admitted sheepishly, averting her gaze from the gravely path that lay before them, with a dainty White House to match the older feel to the customers surrounding.



The air smelled faintly of gas from passerby vehicles, perfumed with the trees clustered around the restaurant.

“It’s…” Zane trailed off, chewing on the words he preferred as his first line of defense, however retained with a bite to his tongue. 

“Not an *unfavorable* atmosphere.” He finished, his admittance making his tone crack, which he immediately regretted to speaking in the first place. In retaliation, Zane fixed his expression rightly into a scowl as though his voice never faltered.



“You know,” she hummed, her fingers drumming on her palm, to which he felt the vibrations of, “if you retain that expression any longer, you just might have it stick that way for the remainder of your depressing noble, high-end life.” She jested, the scowl carving deeper in Zane’s expression.

“Mistress—“

“Even your crowd doesn’t grant me that high a mighty title.” The retort was quick, if Zane didn’t know any better, he’d have mistaken it for *snappy*.

“Rachel.” He caved in, torn between liking to refer to her with simply her surname and the pet name they (or…more accurately, Rachel) decided on.
