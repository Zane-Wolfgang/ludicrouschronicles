Heels of shoes scuffed damp sidewalk as two boys drew a jagged path through the abandoned streets of Farturn.

Night had crowned victor for the hour. Lamps flickered dying gasps of ember as the figures strode past. They did little to keep their voices low, caring not for individuals who may have been caught in deep slumber.

"You're seriously far too pent up." Jackson mirrored Zane. A subconscious effort, as he was an assassin by trade. It was natural for his body to copy the stances of his targets and work in tandem with them in such a way that it's passed off as ordinary.

Jackson continued his tangent.

"So the girl asked if she could see your eyes. That's not something to make a fuss over."

"Drop the conversation, Jackson." Zane abruptly snarled–which was strange in his own right.

Zane, while largely being a spoiled brat. He'd never had the gal to readily respond with a heavy set of emotions, generally deflecting with a roll of eyes or something of the sort. Most importantly, he tended to retain this heavy aura about him. Constantly sulking. But he was no aggressor.

*He* of all people would never *snarl*.



Though it wasn't Jackson's job to know what Zane did or didn't do since he was Rachel's target, he still could pick up on some semblance of moments where the man had dropped his guard. This...was a reaction he'd never predicted.

A touchy subject, eh?

Suddenly, the game seemed much more serious, and the little details Jackson could salvage and hand to Rachel to exploit seemed to loom over his mind, devouring it in greed.

"I hope you didn't just have the gal to *growl* at me, pal." Jackson whipped toward the dirty-blonde, who halted, nose wrinkled.

"And what if I did?-"

*WHAM*!

A startled, sharp jab of pain shot through his rips and crushed his lungs as his back scraped against brick. Something was lodged against his esophagus, and whatever it was steadily applied pressure, making him kick and squirm in reflex and terror.

It took him astoundingly longer than he'd care to admit what was causing the air blockage. Through a hazy exchange between his eyes and his brain, he pieced together what he could make out, and saw an arm extending from a body that wasn't his own.

And a pair of harsh red eyes steadily burning a hole through his.

*Jackson*.



***Oh* shit**.



"Le-*heh*-t... m-Me... *go*..!!" Zane choked out, coughing up spit that ensured his steady path to *breathing* wasn't an easy one. The hairs on his body began to rise, and the delicious feeling of adrenaline already infected his veins.

The countdown had begun.
