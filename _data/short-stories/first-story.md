It was the sweet chill that breathed down Zane’s back that alerted him first of the presence. It was an awareness that remained acute, but none of the less still causing him to stir slightly from the deeper throes of sleep.



“Mmnnghh..?” 

The only sound he could muster was that, followed by a hiss as the cold was soon followed by adrenaline, kissing his pulse and quickening his heart rate, his eyes widening to the rush.



\*What the-?\*



Suddenly he was out from under the covers, on his knees against the sheets, his eyes narrowing to pinpoints.

\*No. No nonono\* NONO N O!



No sooner had that thought finished, commenced something that scared him so deeply to his roots he thought he was living in a nightmare.

His breath came in gasps as he scrambled—

\*The threads around his neck were tightening.\*

He was choking, grasping at the threads, clawing at them, trying to dig underneath them to leave enough space for gulp down air. 

Whatever it was wouldn’t relent, and he was left sharply inhaling through a windpipe that was slowly crushing against his will.



“L-\*let—…!\*” That sentence alone cost him, he was running out of air, and the delicious adrenaline in his veins pumped more frantically, feeding into a pleasure reeled from the circulation of oxygen cut off.

Zane had to act—\*now\*.

His stomach lifting and sinking frantically, his gaze flitted around the room like a butterfly, his gaze not even a moment later locking on the pair of scissors he’d borrowed for a project he intended to make with parchment.



Dizzy but motivated, his line of sight drawing dark, he stumbled to the desk, collapsing ontop of it and barely managing to flip himself over to easily cut into the threads.

But it hadn’t occurred to him, as he was guiding the dual blades under the first coils of thread… that around his neck happened to be the strongest material in the world…and scissors were no match.

Under the drunk haze of exhaustion, adrenaline, and lack of oxygen, he hadn’t realized until the third attempt at snipping them off did it occur to him.



\*\*\*SHIT.\*\*\*



And through the constant bombarding of his senses, he hadn’t realized the threads had loosened enough for him to continue living, nearly drinking up the air as he perused further with the scissors, trying to cut them with futility. Each snip more vigorous than the last as he roared with rage, instead poising the blades at his bare throat—



…just to hear something \*clatter\* to the floor.

Zane looked up.

He…

When did he drop the scissors?

And he could have sworn he was halfway across the room, now.

The moon that poured in from his window and spilled across the bed was no longer near his feet.

What…

What just happened…?

And why did he feel a terror, separate to his own, about the thought of death?
