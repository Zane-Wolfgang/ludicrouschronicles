“Dearest Eniaz,” a voice, smooth as velvet, sinful as the devil sang its way into his ears, followed by the sly tail slithering across marble behind.

“Does thou wish to accompany his maiden to outer quarters?” She prompted, a mane of brilliant sapphire black smoke spiraling off her, shrouding the rippling muscles underneath. “For I have a gift thou surely would most appreciate.”

A select set of eyes narrowed, teal amidst the dark, spearing the ground with such a gaze. Once lifted, they softened upon settling over animalistic features.

“But of course.” A rumble stewed from his throat, spinning a cocoon of warmth right around the beast, causing her to shudder from delight.

He strokes his chin, expression morphing somewhat skeptical, though any further than that would be considered an anomaly.

“At this hour is most unusual, however.” He injects, though her many eyes lift in glee, the blue fire blasting at the tip of her tail perking up in unison, before the flame tapered and relaxed.

“Thou will trust, thou will bathe in its glory. Follow thee.” She went on a delightful little skip, claws scraping the marble but not so much as leaving a skid underneath.

“For how far do our travels fair?”

“Not long.” Her tail whisks, Eniaz lets the flame brush his face, warmth rolling off his skin—for a god, he need not feel pain, nor will he experience it. He’s vowed much the same to his maiden before him—an unnamed specimen, one neither have thought up a name for. She simple was. And thrived. One day, when the sky was anew, it will arrive to one of them. Until the time comes, Eniaz simply refers to her as mistress.



It was a long while before they left the throes of black marble columns and twisted carved stone of the castle, the high domed ceiling giving way to sky. Marble underfoot melted to stone as the archway revealed.



It was…no longer the blank expanse of the void, a gaping maw of death for those who flew past the borders the world set for them. Nay, it was blanketed.

By no less than *diamonds*.

Hundreds upon thousands glimmered across the sky, though it were a pond filled with pebbles. Despite an eternity with space above, no man could dream of counting them.

“My wonder…” Eniaz began, not tracking his expression. Furthermore, he’d been missing the beasts smug expression, sitting her lower half down to admire the gods guard as it dropped.

“Thou has never dreamed of such beauty, as thee has reckoned.” With a gentle spin, talons delicately lifted his own claws, squeezing with caution but further with delight.

“They, as now, is thou’s.”

Eniaz’s brows lifted, a sharp breath sucked through his fangs. “What has thou created?” He pondered despite the words he wished to say roiling in his stomach, refusing to show their heads.

“Diamonds unlike any other.” One talon made itself vacant to cast dramatically to the sky. “For it is night, and these are thou’s stars.”

“Night,” he followed, his eyes diverting from the spectacle. Nothing retained more interest in this blasted world more than this creature, the one he called his own, his *equal*. “A concept most fascinating. Would thou tell more?”

“Blazes.” Her answer a purr that vibrated through his bones, comforting him like a fire in the winter. “I pulled thee from the dark and brought fourth the fire to light thou’s way.” Her tone sounded haunted, but mystifying, according to Eniaz. 

The ‘night’…was something he could barely fathom its beauty for. And it was thanks to *her*.

“Cosmos and solars, planets and lifeless expanse as galaxies.” Eniaz let his mind pull what his beast had been thinking. “Thou has spent long for such a dream.” He floats, now, blue threads slithering out from behind his neck as he took her talon as if offering a dance in the sky.

“Let our travels take us to the stars.” Much of what he said was lackluster, lost in those multiple burning blue eyes, a soft sigh escaping his lips. “Would you care for a dance?”



Soon, their waltz, despite what should have been most odd to onlookers, was most magnificent in their claws.

Mistress gazed adoringly at the lover she cradled, letting him sweep her up steps that never existed, climbing higher into the sky.

“I gift thee, stars above, every last ember. Would thou take such a gracious gift?”

“With talons and teeth.” He swore, bringing their heads together.

“What does thou wish to do with so many flames?”

“Thee shall make them more. A wonder unbeknownst to the eye.” He replied swaying them to spiral upward further.

“Simply gaze. All the rest will fall to place.”



The beast could barely pry her head away from her lover, and blinked, following his order.

Her tail twined around his waist as he flicked his wrist, then waving a semi-circle across the sky.

In an instant, the stars…

They *exploded*.

Shimmers of cerulean, magenta, violet streaked the sky, streaming with hues of pulsating gold shoring off each throes edge. The blast reached to meet them with a gentle gust, nothing more than that.

The sound followed was shrill but quiet, destruction as a bell toll while a cannonball crashed nearby.



She hovered in awe, her eyes sparking with shine Eniaz had rarely found so prominent, but yet he remained still, watching his darling rather than his own-made spectacle.

“T’was thou’s ingenious idea?”

“Conceived from mere thought about thou’s marvelous gaze upon thee.”

“Young mistress,” he crows, letting her coo in response to a title. “Thou lay far superior than these stars. If thou were to find herself amidst silence, hail thee with a nova.”

Her arms wound around his back, claws scraping his chest as she caved in around him, burbling happy as the crack of dawn shown over the peaks, bathing them in rich gold for morning again.
