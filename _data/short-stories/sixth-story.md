“And you, Zane…” An old man’s nimble fingers set a box into the dirty-blondes outstretched palms. “The will grants you this. A warning…displaced here…” The old man trailed off, adjusting his glasses as he mumbled under his breath. “Ah yes,” he uttered wispily, “you may only activate the voice box under dire circumstances. Should your life be in danger, an angel from above will snatch you from the darkest of clutches.”



Zane’s expression faltered as his eyes hovered over the voice box. A small little thing, black, with a speaker and a button on top of it. It was no larger than an alarm clock—come of which was a new contraption, and highly in fashion as of late.

“A… recorder?” He prompted, feeling his excitement drain from him. He’d expected something grander, out of his family. One could only imagine, but he didn’t let his mind stray from the possibilities.

And..

He got…

A voice recorder.



Zane tried not to *tch* too loud, however he found himself rather unlucky since the old man could miraculously pick it up—or that’s what Zane convinced himself, because his next plunge of advice felt rather out of the blue.

“Be not woeful, young one, for I find this gift specially tailored to the likes of yourself. Fortune finds you its master, and times like these you must learn to embrace it.”

With that, the old man swept away in his robes, and Zane watched him go, fingers curling tight over the recorder, nails raking its plastic surface.



\= = = = =



Zane finds himself later lounging on Rachel's couch, tossing the recorder in the air, a bored scowl etched into his features as if he were a statue.

*Fwuip*,

catch.

*Fwuip*,

catch.

The cycle repeated until a door unlocked from behind him. Which, mind you, could have been and hour, and Zane likely wouldn't have noticed the passage of time.

"Hey."

"Greetings, Jackson." Zane mimed with an equally bored tone Jackson bore, continuously tossing the contraption in the air, hoping that it might change it's form if he did it enough.

"Nice to see you're infuriatingly formal as ever." Replied Jackson, making his way around the couch to slink into a cabinet with some thinly sliced potatoes Rachel baked the day previously.

"What do you have there?"

"Erm...a recorder of sorts, by the looks of it."

"Any practical use?"



Zane then stopped tossing the device, propping himself up on his elbows, hoisting his upper half up to provide Jackson with a healthy... scornful look. 

"No." He let himself say at last, just in case if Jackson, who retained a rather thick skull, couldn't judge his response from his expression.

"Then why do you have it in your possession?" After gauging Zane's expression he treaded lightly, since his companion seemed in a rather...negative mood, or at least he assumed. Jackson then slid his fingers under a glass container, snatching it up and leveling it gently onto the marble counter, unlatching its cover to pick one of the crisped slices of potatoes.

"...It was a gift, or so I understand it." Zane audibly sighed, slouching before tossing himself back onto the cushions, resuming his game of tossing his device up into the air.

"Then it's a decoration?" Jackson reasoned, snapping the crisp in half, chips raining against his shirt, bluntly remaining ignorant until he noticed a rather large chunk filtering to the floor. What he could salvage, he threw into the sink.



"I have no clue." Zane inspected the recorder, eyes narrowing to the series of dots that served as its speaker. "He informed me I should use it when I'm in great danger though." He left out the section where an *angel above* will snatch him...from...clutches of...darkness, was it? Something that could bring him harm. Very little of which he cared about.



\*Nothing my threads can't handle.\* He didn't say that aloud either, however. "Otherwise, he urged me not to listen to it." That brought back his sour mood, his fingers itching to claw at the mechanism in his clutches.

"So...I guess I won't."



The sentence alone made him hurdle the damn thing at the end of the couch, watching it bounce against the arm rest and settle somehow between his thigh and one of the cushions.

"Tch."

Jackson eyed the contraption, bit another one of the crisps, and stashed away the remaining baked potato slices, waddling over to the couch.

"Theoretically, there'd been no mention of someone else listening for you." He inquired, curling his fingers around the device and bringing it up to his eyes to observe it.

"There's always that." Zane agreed, his eyes lidded with a rather distasteful expression. "On the contrary, there's a possibility sensitive information lies within that box. If you somehow got your mitts on information strictly meant for myself and my family, heaps of trouble will follow you wherever you reside."



Jackson's brow furrows as he spares his gaze towards his friend. "Rather interesting prospect... but it's rather silly to imagine. You're in danger, possibly about to lose your life...and here's some information about a trust in the middle east we kept safely hidden with a shareholder that lives in the underground."

Zane flinched.

"...You can't tell me I happened to get that right."

"You were close." Zane hesitantly admitted sheepishly. 

Jackson didn't press further. 

"Well, decide. I don't plan standing around for you to drum up an answer 'til next Christmas."

"...I relent. You have a point for once in your burgluring, and rather depressive life. Do what you will." He waved Jackson off, who happily bounded into the next room, eager to hear some secrets as the door slid shut.

Zane had been so adamant about not listening to whatever lie behind the speaker, so being a candidate to possible secrets was thrilling.

Eagerly, his thumb hovered over the button, anticipation bubbling in his gut. His hair was on end, and with a slow push, the speaker sparked to life.

The man prepared to hear some dark secret spill, just in the off-chance his off-handed comment happened to be a spark of truth...



"**Wake up**."

He stilled, his movements stuttering.

Did he hear that right?

"That can't be...correct." He mumbled, rubbing his chin before pressing the button again (it would have been a rather large shame had it been a one-time use, to be fair. Explaining that to Zane probably would have earned him a good wallop).

The same message crackled through the speaker. Jackson frowned.



*That's it?* He thought, jiggling the handle to let himself out of the room, eyeing the device in his hands with disinterest.

Yet as he stepped through, an air of wariness wafted directly from the couch—Zane.

"So...? Was it anything important...?"

Jackson, despite wanting to pull his friends leg, kindly held back against his will.

"Um... if you consider the two words a disembodied voice uttered to me in the most blandest fashion possible important..." He lingered on the tail-end of the sentence, figuring out how else to phrase that, but drawing up a blank slate.



Zane snorted. "What, is it 'you're adopted' or something?"

Jackson, unable to withhold his urge to screw with his friend for even a little bit, morphed his expression into something remarkably hesitant.

Zane's amused facade dropped instantly. "Wait, what?"

After a moment, the black man cracked, before cackling like a man guilty. "Oh come on! Learn to take a joke. It was nothing of the sort." Jackson tossed back the contraption to Zane, who didn't catch it, letting it fall next to him on the cushions. Not to mention, he was giving his friend a healthy glare.

"...I told you, it was merely *jest*..." Jackson grumbled, side-eyeing the wall since it bore no resentment toward him at the proper moment.

"This is why I don't spend my valuable time with slimy gits like you." The dirty-blonde countered, digging his nails into the crevices of the device.

Jackson, promptly ignoring such a blatant insult, bit the inside of his cheek. "It was strange though."



"What was?" Zane didn't spare him a glance.

"The sentence."

"Mhm?"

"It was something you'd hear every day." Jackson scratched his scalp, his dreads swishing wildly around as he leveled his gaze with his friend. "It was—"

"Don't tell me." Zane hastily interrupted, brow furrowed. "If I was meant to hear it during dire times, then that's when I'll allow myself to activate the recorder." He lumped the mechanism onto a nightstand beside the couch, leaning his head on the armrest.

"It was just 'wake up'." Jackson relinquished, rolling his eyes. "That's no such sentence to tell someone in a time of need." He spun on his heel, striding away from the couch, deciding he could probably stomach some deer meat if they had any left in the fr—



The man stopped.

Dead in his tracks.

He shuddered.

Jackson had actively *shivered*.

Full-body, *death is looming over you*, kind of shiver. His nerves froze over, every hair on his body stood on end. Dread washed over him. Fear inking his limbs, screaming at him to **run**.



A voice, the British lilt still tainting, yet silky smooth and demonic all the same, slithering through the residence sunk into Jackson.

"***I recall politely asking you to refrain from informing me what exactly the contraption contained.***"

Jackson jolted so hard he slammed Rachel's refrigerator door without noticing, choking on his own breath.

He hadn't dared turn around.

He was *terrified*.

"***And yet, here I stand, awake by none of my own means, with not an ounce of hazardous interaction nearby?***"



*W-... what the... h-hell?* Jackson's *thoughts* were stuttering. If he could force his head to glance down, his limbs would retain the same stutter his mind had. He couldn't even come up with a dumb response, as all the air hit a block in his throat, and refused to advance further into his lungs.

He's still shaking.

All he know's is he's **going to die.**
