Ever since the first infection, Rachel and I have been inseparable.
It was to lessen the casualties. That's what we convinced each other as we roamed the streets of infected.
Buildings were weathered, remained on fire from the chaos that spilled from the residents that never made it out alive, burnt marks scathing the ground...bullet holes in the concrete. Blood smeared across pavement that used to be filled with people.
A barren wasteland met any survivor that hasn't taken his life from the sheer insanity alone. If you had a strong enough will to withstand, you were to seek others of your own immediately. Even through groups with turmoil between the clashes of opposing teams, they had to set aside their differences to remain standing among the infected that sought to destroy civilization from the inside out.
We, amongst no other, were the only ones of our kind, Rachel and I. Our parents were never mentioned on any records, and we were most certainly not siblings. Strange powers possessed our bodies, as we'd come to learn, siding the fact *we were immune to the infected.*
Rachel was immensely helpful in ways I would have never been able to last through. Nearing photographic memory, abilities to locate objects to break us out of tight spots, reflexes unlike no other.

Yet at times at night, my body shakes. I've seen so many of those I'd cared about.
Fall.
Where I had to raise my own gun and shoot them without tearing away my gaze. Nay, *our* gaze.
Where we had to watch those we loved perish twice, once by the demons that plagued the world, and lastly, our own two hands.

Zane fears. (Rachel's voice for the moment.)
Rachel fears worse, breaks worse. I've had to keep her together long enough to help us find solutions. Exits. Roundabouts.
The close calls were excruciating.
Near death experiences were a daily occurrence.
We hated it.
Every morning, when we woke up, we wondered if today was going to be our last.
When we looked in the mirror, we always expected an infected to appear behind us and make us vanish like all the others.
Death was always...*always* around the corner. 

And naturally, ever since Rachel had possessed me, my body has seen some significant changes. Though most I could obscure under clothing; the stray feather, fingers lengthening to claws, smoke wafting off my square shoulders when I got pissed.
Not to mention our *minds* merged. A catastrophe, I know. We somehow managed.
I'd never told her how much I feared what would happen if we seperated. I think I'd lose my mind. When she goes to sleep I allow myself to think these selfish thoughts, grabbing at my arms with my claws. *What if one day she abandons me?
What if she just gives up one day?
What if she uses my gun to kill me? Or worse, herself?
What if she* disappears?
I couldn't grasp sanity. What little I could scrape was talking to myself, taking turns of switching seats with Rachel.
We didn't seperate. We *didn't seperate.*
Throughout the masses of destruction.
From the hoards of keys we had to collect to escape mazes of puzzles and riddles.
To escape the mobs that sought to bury their weapons into our body.
They wanted us *dead.*
But we were a few players on a chessboard.
How much further will our rabbit hole go?
