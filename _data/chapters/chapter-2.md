The conductor was slowly dragging the locomotive to a halt, its wheels screeching noisily beneath their compartment.\
"You’ve never ceased to amaze me.” Those words left a sour taste in Zane’s mouth, but he forced them out all the same, simultaneously folding his book and tucking it away in his back pocket. “As you’ve predicted, we’ve arrived at your precisely given time frame." He brands that with a glance at his watch. 

Normally, he would have been rather more forward with his wealth and flaunt it with fashionable accessories—a gold watch or a diamond ring, for instance. However, whether he wanted to stand out or remain under the radar, he detested the weight of such luxury items, which was incredibly hampering his dignity to say the least..

Though, this watch *did* brandish a few diamonds, so as not to cramp his style with the bland black leather binding. It carried an interface of charcoal and gold. While it would do, its use was more siding on what it could provide him rather than just an everyday accessory.

“I simply do my job, Sir.” A beat, where blue eyes met crystal. After the brief, she moved, as if to offer a consoling gesture—but she jutted to a halt and veered back, deciding against it last-minute.

\
“… E-excuse me. We should… We should get going, Sir.” A tiny, awkward chuckle tumbled from the depths of Elijah’s throat, barely audible had Zane not been paying attention to the best of his ability.\
“I agree, … let’s depart.” He managed, his stand leisurely and measured, remaining a slight stroke of skepticism. 

Voices flooded the halls beside them, dulled around the edges since the mass of commoners lay opposite their door. It didn’t distract him from the way her gaze lingered on his face, before she swiftly swung down to glance underneath her, barely managing to drag forth her briefcase from beneath the seat. She undid the latch with her elbow and shouldered the aged door, which shuddered (Zane suspected those who took after the train were lacking in their department, or else this wouldn't have been noticeably noisy). A feat of skill followed by a sprinkle of laziness (which Zane could atone many lifetimes for), after then Elijah lumbered down the hall.

The dirty-blonde let himself stray behind, adjusting his position at an awkward angle to grapple onto his luggage, surprisingly in the same position he left it since beginning his journey from Brooksville to Farturn.

\
Speaking of Farturn… the area, or so he heard, was rather the opposite of Brooksville, which was a rural town. It was kept in the crook of Connecticut, semi-secluded and neatly kept, roughly a population of 100,000 residents or more within. It felt a lot more lively, contrasting the weather about.\
Despite the town, it had more a… valley feel, considering the plethora of trees with mountains encasing it. And yet, with the town being a rather large landmark, with the occasional passerby tourists, not many traversed through the area. It was easier to avoid, unless you sought after the colors during the change in season.\
Which is precisely why many might be visiting at this time.

Zane nipped his shades’ edge to sling them over his face, obscuring his glowing eye from prying leers of the public—or what he deemed, *the unworthy*. The latter title was more of a boost of confidence for himself, however, as he merely wished to remain as inconspicuous as possible while also being a celebrity around these parts. A simple plan for a complicated man.

“Sir Zane!” Chirped Elijah.\
He grumbled under his breath as he hauled his suitcase to his side, his arm sagging from the weight as he settled the thing behind him with an exaggerated huff. He suppressed the cold that was strangely insistent in his veins down to a slight chill.\
“Coming.”\
Without further ado, Zane was off, bustling behind Elijah (careful not to tread on her heel and remain a respectful distance), occasionally sparing a glance at his cuffs branding what was largely his lower forearm, buttoned by silver. 

Not to mention his hand currently remained vacant from the ring that usually bore his family's crest… though he was in no position where it was critical for the accessory to be present, he had inevitably tucked it away in his suitcase pocket on the outer shell.\
But that was a prior decision, and he was starting to feel rather odd without the familiar weight on his finger.\
So he let himself have a small debate on whether he’d unpack it, pride soon crowning victor over the war as he unzipped and slipped the accessory onto his middle finger. He quickly recovered and straightened his posture.

*Better safe than sorry, as they say. Shan’t have problems with identification this afternoon… should there be any.*

The medallion on his chest would have been fine enough proof. Maybe it was just an act of soothing the strange ripple of wariness in his stomach.

“This way, Sir Zane!” Elijah chimed, happily kept her briefcase dipping as low as her knees (rather a shocking revelation she hadn’t packed more than he, however she was summoned on short notice, and he supposed that gave applicable reason), which ended up thumping loudly against her thighs when she took off, trotting happily off the exit way leading down off the train and into a narrow canal. Zane brought the corner of his lip up to a disgusted snarl, his nose scrunching.

\
“Eugh… More commoners? I’d rather stick my nose in my father’s quarters and receive a beating than have to deal with a mess such as this.” He proclaimed, eyeing with distress over the mass of limbs all squashing to get off the train.\
A surprising number of people wanted to visit this sorry excuse for a town—it hadn’t offered anything special besides the lovely fall colors.\
Well, anything special to anyone except *Zane.*

Because what he needed here, he was certain he couldn’t attain anywhere else, which was—

With a grand, unnecessary gesture, Elijah, his secretary of all people, looped her arm around his, taking in a deep breath big enough for the both of them.

\
“We’re here!” She had the audacity to announce to the archways of corroded metal, paint flaking off in areas to depict age. The ceilings were high, more than half consisting of windows that let the afternoon dim gush through. Although white, not a soul would find it difficult to navigate from the lack of light. The rain had shrugged off elsewhere temporarily. Blips of sun somewhat streaked the sky, however, it no longer offered the warmth it once did in the summer months. The partially added cloud cover boxed some warmth for them, at the very least.

\
*Sigh* Well… Zane supposes he can soak up the sun while he can. It proves useful to the human body, after all..\
His black dress shoes’ heels clicked against the smoothed stone beneath him, an elated platform descending toward a maze to await patiently to board. Luckily, he was taking an exit that didn’t demand such elaborate turns and constantly weaving through to escape, but rather one narrow pathway that was clogged by the rush of people that just left the train alongside him.

“Poverty is simply bringing this culture down to hell, and honestly, let them be damned.” He clipped sourly, growling to the line. He’d have to wait, which is not what he paid for. It was rather unfortunate that the town was *just* small enough as to not allow private trains along these tracks. Really, it irked him—terrible misfortune for the dirty-blonde to be caught up in such meandering troubles.\
“Now now, Sir,” chided Elijah, as if she were tutting a child, “just be patient. As we were one of the last off… Tis as per your request.”\
“…Right.” He looked down at his vest, which remained unbuttoned near the top, revealing a white tie. He absently tugged upon it with a thumb, loosening it by a hair. He could feel the musk of the sea of people plaguing him and his clothes, which made him terribly uncomfortable. He wished he had an out to unveil his distaste for the area, particularly centered on the idea of uncovering a deep scowl, but he fought against it. He’d rather set his focus more on the direction they should head.

Tan stone pillars surrounded them, supporting those brown metal arches—this place really felt as if it were composed of metal poles and smooth, beige stone, nothing more articulate than that. It’s as if the designers of this place thought it would be far too extravagant to add so much as a breath of furniture, even if it was a singular potted plant.

*What a hoax*—he scolded himself for setting standards high.

"I find this a grand place. Wondrous work, if you will." Elijah states, happily swaying in her place. "What say you?" Always the ever-so-positive one, wasn’t she? Attempting to keep spirits high.\
Zane glanced back once at the train. Steam once rolled from the train's smoke stack, but it's long since evaporated.\
"It's shabbier than I'd have hoped for." He relented, letting his eyes spin counter-clockwise in exaggeration. Currently, his primal desire urged him to seek out a bed to lounge upon and let his back rest from the agony of remaining slouched for a portion of the ride here.\
He twisted his head back to Elijah, who looked rather a tad put out. 

He felt no sense of remorse in the way her downcast expression shunned his snide; it only propelled him further with the instinct to relieve himself from the station as swiftly as possible.

"I misunderstand as to why you find discomfort in such an exquisite structure, Sir." She laments, her tone grading down in volume as she trained her line of sight elsewhere.

*And* I *simply cannot fathom how you don't believe this place is a haven for tasteless individuals*.  His mind grated on. *Wagering on such a poorly constructed station was a waste of expense.*

He doesn't say it aloud, gathering his thoughts as he angled down the steps conjoined with an elaborate pathway down for the disabled, complete with rough and gritty handrails disguised under rust. Zane tried not to grimace as his palm scraped against the offering metal, quick to shy his hand away from its texture.

*Especially considering your heritage… what in the suns has gotten into you?* (After a moment of realization that there was, in fact, no such thing as sun’s’, he prided himself on a new idea for a saying.)\
And so, down the path they strolled.\
Picking their way through the crowd as it thinned, they located a broad entryway, yawning open to the departed. Zane perked slightly to the Roman numerals etched into the clock above, the hands ticking away deliberately. It seems this structure was stationed further along than Zane realized, not that it accentuated much.

"I deftly hope knowledge of these routes lies upon thou." He sways, eyes skimming over the crowd. And there it was, just as he suspected would soon arrive. The pinprick feeling of being watched.

*Though rather early… 'twas bound to occur at some point in time.* He thought to himself, keeping the intellect he'd gathered to himself, not at all letting it be obvious to his stalker he was aware of his prying eyes.\
"But of course!" The redhead scrambled, spearing Zane's concentration as she hurried along the path, weaving through the bustle, he not far behind.\
Simultaneously, he busied himself with pinpointing the location of those pairs of eyes.\
As he pressed his thought into it, \*cold power\* stemmed through his veins, adding further chill to his body that the air provided bountiful amounts of.

*No.*\
He flinched, squashing down his power, imagining crushing it beneath his heel. He wished for his power to stay out of his affairs, but …

*I need no help from insolence such as you.* He thinks bitterly to *it,* even if *it* had no voice. No thought, or presence, exactly. It was no other being. Probably. Nonsense such as that was unheard of.

Soft spurs and drums of jazz throughout speakers were drowned by chatter, even if it strayed overhead, pouring onto the mass, where it shouldn't have been promptly disrupted. By gods—he wished he could split the crowd with one, large, reality-defying hand, sweeping people to the side. While it would earn him startled yells and gasps, at least they'd be *silenced…* but he wasn't allowed even *that* luxury amongst his own personal bubble. It irked him. But what could he d—

He clapped a hand on the back of his neck, eyes narrowing to points as he felt the threads threaten to erupt.

Tongue clasped between his teeth, he bit back the urge to release a hiss of pain he caused himself.\
Innately, his power almost answered to his desires, trying to fork through the spaces between his fingers and heed his beck and call.\
"Sir Zane?" Elijah asked, hushed, as to not draw attention, which would be considerably foolish an act whilst in public.

"I'm fine." He gutted out, matching her tone and volume. He kept a stark, borderline unexpressive face, only having that split second of vulnerability.

*All this activity… I'm not accustomed to it in the slightest.* He thinks, finally batting back the urge to use his threads to sweep the premises free of blockage conspired by the people in his wake. *Perhaps, these things, too, sense answers are close.*

Zane raises his head, Elijah having the slightest hint of worry radiating in the draw of her eyes, but, expression-wise, laid majorly clear and unconcerned.\
She was specially trained to not question many of the outbursts Zane had when retaining control, nor show it, especially in the face of the masses. That would wreak havoc, and likely news cast to papers sold all around the region. 

So the less problems shown between rich folk like him, the better. The fact he hasn't been swarmed didn't necessarily mean a great deal to him. In fact, it was welcoming, especially in a place where he was less recognizable. Thank the suns. Perhaps his getup was aiding him in that regard..\
\
They walked underneath the grand clock, in the bustle of the main section of the station. Traffic slipped neatly in between one another, fluid as water. Not many stopped to tend to anything save for those who came to greet friends or family of the like.

*But never mind that… I've yet to locate those pairs of eyes…* 

Realization struck that he no longer felt the gaze. It was as if the presence had completely *dissipated.* Understandably, it got him questioning.\
There was no guard stationed by his hip to ensure his protection, so, virtually, he was exposed. 

Well, of course… save …For his *threads.* If it was a threat, he had to prepare, in any case—\
Before his thoughts spiraled further…\
“By the trees, I’m going to miss the train!” A voice squeaked behind Zane, who whirled around just in time to–

*… Bump.*

*Oh–*

His eyes snagged against black hair adorned with frosty tips, framing the face of a woman his age. Shorter than him, by a considerable margin, half a head or so.\
She hadn't been paying attention, unsuspecting of a body in her way, and having collided with him. Judging by the way her voice sounded when he first caught a glimpse of it, she appeared to have her head in a different direction.

"Ah-!"\
Head and eyes snapped to him, eyes amber melting into gold, wide and alert as the woman had noticed her mistake, both recoiling in tandem.\
"Oh, damnit all, I'm so sorry—" Despite cursing, her tone sounded as if this were a slip of the tongue. She brushed her trench coat, flinching. "I've been paying attention elsewhere, please, forgive me—"\
But Zane didn't hear that at all. His grip tore at the saturated blue suspenders against his hips, eyes wide. Paralyzed, he remained.

*Warmth.*\
That's all he could feel. As if it were no longer the cold brush of autumn against his skin.\
Whatever ice had tarnished him fled his systems… all of it, replaced with underlying warmth, roots of heat spreading like ivy underneath his skin.\
His concentration strained, unavailable to anything but the girl in front of him.

He wouldn’t have even noticed her approach if she hadn’t called out so blatantly.

So his mind sputters, briefly, a slight undertone of panic seeping in. Not to mention, Zane felt oddly… oddly *nostalgic* about the feeling.

Needless to say, he was completely, and utterly unaware that the woman let her eyelids draw back in the same surprise that mirrored his own.

All it took was the slightest touch. All of it, all in the span of a moment. Then… Time sped up. He hadn't even realized it had decreased speed.\
Her gaze, partly concealed by honeyed-gold and ink colored bandages, had started inspecting him curiously.  Yet *she* was the one that demanded attention with such a strange attire, especially for a woman.

He crested his gaze to her, flinching.\
"Y-yes," he stammered, unrelenting despite Elijah cursing out the girl in French, trying to tell Zane not to be tardy… Something… See, he was unable to patch a thing together as to what she said. He had no necessity to pay attention to Elijah at the proper moment.\
"I'm… sorry too." All the dirty blonde could do was flounder, distracted by her voice—It sounded as if she was attempting to slip under his guard, to disarm him with a lustful, syrupy tone.\
It had his heart firmly in its grasp with no way of letting go.\
"I'm—ye-es."\
Bah, conversing was for peasants, anyway. So he let his eyes do the talking for him… though, rather unintentionally.

Zane could list all of the things he found conventionally attractive about her. The gentle caress of her face rounded elegantly toward her chin, the sharp gaze in her eyes that appeared too warm. Her breasts, golden frills spilling over a dark dress-shirt, that of which was concealed by a brown trenchcoat.\
Long, black, gloves shredded at the finger which were lacking fabric drawn to the elbow, laced with small ribbons to secure them. Gray pants, leading one's eye to her boots, shark ouroboros hooked around straps brought up from said trenchcoat to her footwear, just above the knee.\
\
He glanced back after realization dawned on him. Those hadn’t been any kind of natural footwear.\
Her legs were made of parts: metal plates, pistons, springs. Orange-tipped toes curled to appear as talons from a bird of prey, yellow bulbs just below the knee that gave off a soft glow.\
They were *prosthetic.*

*Her legs—what happened to her?*\
He refused to let his mind catch up on whatever he just witnessed, kicking himself into third gear to escape the situation. He had a bed to attend to.\
“I’m—a-afraid I must get going.” Zane blurted, his brows drawn together in heaps of confusion. It was so *difficult* to de-scramble his thoughts. What was \*happening\* to him?\
*Focus on anything but her breasts,* he begged his mind, growing dizzy from overdrive.\
Right, distraction—he’s… the legs! Yes… He’d never so much as laid eyes on a citizen disabled to such a degree before, much less a woman. This was an entirely new \*premise\* to him. He was used to men with missing flesh at best—those who had fought in war. He was flabbergasted by that. Certainly only that. Nothing more.\
\
*What am I* doing? *Why am I stalling this woman? I have things to take care of.*\
“Oh, I–Of course!” She yelps, sliding out of the way, bowing her head.\
“My… my apologies, sir! H-have a good day!” She almost seemed to sputter out the last sentence, her gaze expertly avoiding him in a way that felt oddly similar to how he felt.\
\
*Oh gods. I’m a mess. I’ve never had such a poorly orchestrated interaction in my* life. He felt dizzy.\
\
“I’ve never encountered such a troublesome soul. I hope that girl makes her train.” Elijah quips, a marvelous one-eighty to her previous cursing. Zane sighs, rubbing his face with his left hand. Instinctually, he braced for the rigid cold feeling of his ring against his eyelid, only to freeze.\
*It wasn’t there.*\
\
He remained still for a brief moment, cracking his eyes to ensure the object wasn’t there before a meltdown bubbled up in his stomach, coating his tongue in acid.

“Oh no.”\
His eyes tore open as he glossed his eyes over his middle finger.\
**Gone.**\
His head jerked back up—\
\
Most civilians would generally expect himself not to have noticed his ring slip off and backtrack, searching every possible nook that it might have rolled off to.

But Zane was a rather keen individual.\
His ring fit him perfectly. *It wouldn’t simply ‘fall off’.* Nor had he ever had an instance of that occurring once in his many years of carrying it.

“She stole my ring,” he spoke breathily, bewildered, glancing back to the spot where she was traversing.

“What?” Elijah asked gently, reaching toward him… not that he’d paid attention to her. His eyes snapped up.\
The woman, almost out of sight, but the whisk of her trenchcoat sparked unbridled fury in Zane. *He could catch up.*\
“HEY!” He roared, shoving out of the way of Elijah, who keened with shock, returning down the path he’d come, bolting around all the stanchions forged of gold and looped with velvet red rope.\
“COME BACK HERE, YOU BLOODY THIEF!”

\
*How had he not noticed?* That was the question that revolved in his mind as he tracked her down, people parting in bewilderment.\
*When had the pads of her fingers slipped off something so precious to him?*\
The woman had detected the man gaining on her—and she jolted, as though she hadn’t been expecting this.\
\
*Good. Let fear overcome you.* \
Zane’s thoughts buzzed, the cold trembling through his veins, which he desperately ignored with a shake of his head. He was there, nearly there, a few feet away as she realized she’d halted from being caught so horrendously off-guard, went to resume her act of fleeing.\
\
His arm was just around hers, just there—but of *course* following his success, his powers had the worst timing, slamming his head with a field of clouds, threads snaking down the woman’s head like a halo.\
*By gods, who cares about green threads? I’m not about to have my ring stolen by some–*\
He froze.\
*You’re joking.*\
\
He thought he was going mad.\
He blinked. He… he wasn’t mistaking it.\
He was seeing… Blue threads.

Blue *threads.*

Blue threads, threads the color wrapped around his neck, descended over her as if they were a waterfall of diamonds rather than mere strings.\
Zane’s hand closed around hers as he yanked her back. But that was as far as his body scheduled to go, paralyzed by the sight. Something akin to a current ran between them, and by all that was holy, he restrained himself from that warm feeling that insisted on raking his body and reducing him to a calm state all willy-nilly like.

“You—give me—” He sputtered, his brain unable to further concentrate on his task.

All that it focused on were those damned blue threads.\
All this time, there were *blue threads around someone in the world* seizing her figure—

In a bout of fear, the woman shrieked while all of Zane’s thoughts tumbled about, and he didn’t even catch the moment when she reared her robotic leg back, swinging it fourth.

Following that, Zane couldn’t quite recall, but all he knew was blinding white agony, and crumpling to the ground while footsteps both receded and gained on him.\
\
“ZANE!” Yelled a voice he couldn’t quite comprehend, sharp jabs of pain meeting whatever thought he attempted to scrap together.

*Blue.* Was all that revolved in his head as he struggled to get up. This disorientation—what even happened to him?\
“I have you, Sir, please, lean yourself on me.” A woman. Perhaps Elijah.\
Zane let himself trust that, leaning into an offering warmth as his limbs spasmed.

It then occurred to him.\
*The woman landed a direct hit between his legs.*\
He grimaced.\
*How could I have been so* careless?\
It certainly didn’t help that he had more than just this sight to pry into the colors of others, but it coupled with the fact he… well, he hadn’t exactly *considered* it such, but perhaps this was as close as anyone were to predict the future:

He branded this sight of his to predict ‘before’ threads. He’d always referred to them as that in his mind, not offering to inform another soul of his capabilities. Essentially, he could see faint outlines of where threads would direct others movements, and they’d fill themselves in the precedented state soon after.

*So I’ve fumbled on multiple occasions. Splendid news.*

His head drifted up incrementally, not so much as spotting a soul wandering through this pathway, as this was nearing the train he’d just departed from, so no curious eyes had him pinned.

To that, his threads lashed out, before angling downward to the concentrated point of pain that had him writhing. He hissed as a warm feeling spread throughout him, and his body reset, the pain subsiding and slipping away as though it had never existed in the first place. He allowed himself a sigh of utter relief.

*I suppose I should show gratitude to my threads for that*. He let himself meekly admit, watching them slide down his body and mend what had been injured.\
\
“Damnit.” He’d cursed, blinking his eyes up to Elijah, who had concern etched into every fiber of her body.\
Zane drew himself up, ignoring Elijah. He’d scanned the area more thoroughly, but even with his accursed ability, he’d caught no sign of blue.\
\
*She disappeared. It’s as though she was nothing more than smoke.* He winced as the last of the pain ebbed away, and he stood up evenly now.\
“Curses.” He spat.\
“I lost her.”\
The tone wore heavy on him, and he stood stock still as the feeling in his gut—ice cold—overwhelmed him..\
“Not only that, she got away with my ring.”

Stepping off the last ledge of the lengthy staircase consisting of stone and exiting the station—*Kingston* station—only tormented him further. The first thing upon setting foot in this place… and he’d already been ambushed and mugged. To top it off, Zane had done nothing about it to secure about one of the only things on his person he *needed.*

Elijah had reported to authorities what transpired, the two men exchanging glances skeptically, as though it were rather preposterous such events occurred right underneath their noses (*Pigs*, Zane thought). 

So, they’d promised to keep watch and report any findings back to the station, instructing Elijah and Zane to check back frequently for updates. 

As they parted, Zane’s mind spun. But just as his observation before with the smoke ordeal, there was no sign of a woman with that kind of description. 

*Had she really vanished without so much as a trace?*\
\
His threads unraveled under his clothes. It was the only freedom he’d allow them, since their glow would be outdone by the bright clouds above. Nobody would notice the telltale rustle of his clothing, or the slightest of glow writhing underneath through the thinner aspects of fabric. 

They approached the arch entryway where many people were weaving around them, exposing them to the chilly outside. Though it wasn’t much a difference from inside the station, so who was Zane to mention anything?

But regardless, that matters not right now.

\
Because by god was he *pissed.*\
“I’ve arranged a cart ahead of time, Sir,” Elijah declared, tone smothered in dismay, to which Zane fidgeted. Her attempt to steer his train of thought and direct it down a more distracting thought was all for naught. 

“Please worry not, Sir. For we will catch this culprit. Soon, hell will greet her with its gates.” She assured, rather a gruesome image to paint beside a woman appearing so innocent.\
“That woman will most certainly feel the wrath of my threads if I so much as catch a glimpse of that ink-frosted-white hair again.”\
Zane had ensured that vow would come clear across to Elijah, who startled, almost knocking into a low-hanging branch from a planted tree upon the sidewalk. People were milling by in assortments of colors, casting curious or purposeful glances everywhere, some snagging on the two of them.

On another note—\
*Farturn.*\
Their travels had been excruciating, and already off on the wrong foot, as previously stated. Zane’s eyes flitted over the nearby buildings, what was momentarily drenched in an overlay of yellow from the sun, which soon would be cloaked from the public with more clouds. Even through his rippling anger, he found solace in the slow-moving current of the cloud cover, regardless of whether the sun was peeking through or not. One anchor at a time.

*I’ll get my ring back*. And it was a promise he was most inclined to keep.

He let his eyes dip toward the path in front of him: a curved stone-brick-inlaid road looped toward the front of the station, slick with rain. It was built in preparation for those who’d be dropped off.\
Or rather, in this case, picked up.\
Trees ran all along the sidewalk, maples shrouded in reds and gold speckled birch branches that instantly soothed him, a shudder running through his frame that was anything but noticeable.\
All too soon did the thought sputter his lungs alive, instinctively inhaling deep for him.    

Oxygen barreled into his lungs, filling him with a sense of ease.

\
“I’ll be fine.” He reasoned (more to himself), watching a horse-drawn carriage trot toward them, blinders obscuring the collective steeds’ view.\
*Clop clop clop clop… clop. Clop*.\
They slowed to a stop. Sleek fur, one black, the other white. Hooves polished until they gleamed.\
“Magnificent stallions..” Murmured Elijah, as the coachman tipped his hat toward her.

\
Before Zane could fill his mind with an agreement of: *Yes, quite exquisite, actually,* the man guiding the carriage by reigns piped up.\
“Afternoon, madam. I presume you two quite possibly are my clients for the remainder of the evening?” He inclined, to which Elijah nodded.\
The man began to get off the coach box, but Zane stopped him with a hand up to prevent further advancement from the other party.\
“Fret not over us. I’m well able to take it from here. Stay at your seat.” Now, normally, he’d much rather let those he hired to do the heavy lifting, if not wholly their job. Yet, he felt he could gain some form of distraction if he did it himself.\
“Then… by all means.” The coachman sounded just a touch taken aback, though his expression told none of that. In fact, he seemed rather pleased.\
Zane looked away.

Even then, he felt the coachman’s eyes assessing him, before glancing away towards his carthorses.\
\
“After you, Sir.” She jerked her head to the carriage, which was plenty sheltered from the rain— that of course, if it were to start up another round of rainfall. 

“Thank you.” Zane mumbled, after having offered to haul her luggage (after his) into the trunk.\
\
They boarded soon after, and Zane, besides all that rest he had on the train from before, couldn’t help but sink into the plush seats, rolling his head back and letting out an exhausted sigh.\
He glanced back outside as the wheels underneath his feet began to pivot, swaying him whenever it rolled over a stone (which was a constant).\
The clouds returned with a vengeance, making sure to blot out the sun.\
Zane didn’t even bother to pretend he’d minded.

“In the meantime, while authorities scout for her sightings, why don’t we enjoy the view?” Elijah hedged, adjusting her glasses due to the constant rustling of the cart.\
\
*She might have to do that a few more times,* he let himself be amused for a brush of a moment, then letting his gaze hang outside.\
“That’s…. not all a terrible decision, I wish I didn’t have to admit.” He altered his position, twice having to grab at his shades, since his first attempt missed, just to adjust its resting place atop his head.\
“I reckon we can drop our things off, then from there, we make way to a corporation or two that keep tabs on civilians around here.” He informed her, not at all comprehending the statues, various huddled buildings… the few breaches of scenery in the form of parks and road detours, the handful of churches, nor the library they’d passed.

Elijah ignored him. She kept well to her suggestion.                                                                  “Simply extraordinary, isn’t it?” Wheedled Elijah *again*, eyes sparkling behind her lenses. Or perhaps it was light refracting from the outside and setting her glasses agleaming.

“After we finish this thievery ordeal, would you perhaps enjoy some dinner at an establishment?” She proposed earnestly, irking Zane.\
*The likes of scum like you don't have the luxury to ignore those of my stature*. He thought darkly, bitterly mumbling a mockery of her words under his breath.

 He accepted, though reluctantly, not at all in the mood for confrontation. He needed to think.

And in doing just that, his mind wandered. So did his gaze. He may have had no real desire to act upon it himself, but his eyes steadily found the outside, lids fluttering occasionally from exhaustion.\
Buildings crafted of stone from the Victorian era rolled on by. To his satisfaction, the buildings had a breath of English architecture to them, which naturally meant more detail carved into the stone. Unfortunately, some had been partially obscured by trees and shrubs, so few of those he couldn’t describe the features of accurately.\
For the ones he could, he noted that arches were a common entity amongst the buildings, appearing as often as windows separated neatly, column by column. However, less often one would find turrets etched into the blocky makeup of most structures.\
“They appear to lack variety,” he observes frostily, hellbent on directing his frustration on *something* since he’d been denied. The sound of horse hooves sharply rapping against the stone floor beneath filled the silence—Zane inspected a fountain with a grand statue spewing water from afar before Elijah responded.

“The buildings?” Inquired Elijah, cocking her head in curiosity. The pace slowed for a moment under them.\
“The buildings.” He conceded, perching his elbow beside the window.

The edge of his stern demeanor softened during the course of the ride when nothing else was said. Elijah’s delighted, fidgety energy sapped him free of any negativity that dwelled.

\
Before Zane knew it, his eyelids grew heavy, and he dozed off for the remainder of the ride.
