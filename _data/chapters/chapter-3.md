***Zane…***\
He twisted in his sleep. It was that voice. That voice that had haunted him over and over again.\
**Zane…**                                                                                                                                         

 His body refused to heed to the cold coil in his stomach that was rapidly spreading.                                                           

***Zane… wake u—*** 

“Zane!” Startled, Zane had jerked, his ruffled hair over his eyes, shades clattering to the space between his legs. He was up, he was up! What was all that *ruckus?*\
An exasperated Elijah met his gaze, shifting her endless waterfall of red curls over her shoulder. She allowed herself a sheepish smile to greet him with.

“We’re here.” A subdued voice followed. The tone matched the face, but… the voice itself didn’t come from her.\
“Mrrrhh..?” Zane had groaned in retaliation, gingerly pressing his index finger and thumb to his eyes. How long had he conked out for?                   

\
*…No matter.*

He grabbed his sunglasses and slotted them over his nose, blinking back the abnormally dark navy blue hue (more prominent in his left eye than his right) the lenses provided. Zane turned his attention to the outside.

The coachman had apparently broadcasted their arrival numerous times—er, Elijah, relayed to him. As this had been said, the very same coachman was casting a glance into the carriage as he teetered on the edge, ensuring that his passengers could make him out.

“You had slept so soundly, my good sir.” The woman before him chastised with a jaunty sway of her shoulders, the sentence not matching the way she spoke—though of course, she soon started giggling in the midst of her scolding, essentially breaking her own spell.

“Come.” An exceptionally soft demand, yet a demand all the same.

\
Exceptionally grumpy, but still retaining to his formality (by keeping his temper and attempting to feign politeness, that was), Zane watched as Elijah worked open the cart's door, and cautiously stepping down the ledge.\
Zane realized too late that he hadn’t acted upon etiquette, as he had not only refrained from opening the door for her, but he also failed at assisting her down.\
\
*I suppose that’s not exactly an issue I need to solve anymore.* He huffed. 

*Oh well,* he allowed himself to admit, sweeping off the carriage—”Oh, Sir Zane, please watch your step, it’s sli–” — which was a warning inherently devised to ensure his undoing, as that almost led him to his demise.                                                                                                                   

As one would imagine, he prayed victim to the slippery pebbles under his feet, and nearly found himself flat on his posterior had he not wrestled his balance back from the handle along the carriage door.\
“Sir Zane!” She yelped, to which he adjusted himself swiftly, shooting her a glare. A beat of silence passed between them.\
“...*Perhaps* a warning a*head* of time would have been more thoughtful, *yes*?” He reprimanded, thinking nothing of it when the woman shut the door behind him. She offered her apologies in a startled rush of words, which Zane took no desire to register. So, Zane did what he did best, and trudge along to the back of the cart, not at all paying mind to the house he’d rented for the meantime would look like yet, focused solely on collecting luggage.\
It was only when he hauled his after Elijah’s had he noticed they’ve been ridden to a clearing in the woods.\
Past Elijah’s gawking form, lay the estate. 

Made largely of dark wood with slices of stone, it, on the outside, appeared a cozy cottage. 

The roofs’ black, gritty shingles were uneven and almost thoroughly mismatched, the wooden-edged windows partially warped from hefty amounts of rain. Clipping said windows were barn door shutters on hinges, no longer operatable, but still added to the look.

A cozy veranda was built out front, complete with hanging vines from pots chained along the outer perimeter wooden support beams of the structure, coupled with a chained swing bench along the corner.\
The structure itself was three levels tall and rather sound, the top of the building ending in a rather grotesque shape such as the end of an acorn shell, but not quite rounded off, since it was simply the interface..\
Zane rounded the corner to ensure every vertices of the estate bore no issue, preparing to commit any semblance of mess to memory.

On it’s left beside the dainty house a pond rested, shrouded by ferns and tall stalks of grass. The trench had been dug out by humans, its contents—namely fish—swam placidly around in circles, occasionally blipping to the surface to snack on insects who hardly breached the water, making not so much as a quiver to disturb the stillness.                                            

The wall which faced this pond grew a beard of ivy that ran down and up the wood, dappled with fat leaves that speckled the water below with shadow. The wall of ivy bore a neighbor—a crease had been formed in the wood from a turret jutting out of the house and overlooking this very pond, reflections so absolute from the cloud cover it was difficult to take a peek of what resided within.                                                                                                                                

Strangely, other than that, Zane had noticed, any force of nature clinging to the residence appeared trimmed. Maintained, if you will, as though the growth dwelling along the house completed the overall appearance of it.\
He considered it strange that the land-owners hadn’t claimed back the estate from the brush so it wouldn’t weasel roots in the walls, but… he appreciated the aesthetic it waved off, so he minded little.\
After all, he’d only be renting this estate for a short while, so be there a problem to rear it’s head in his near future, he wouldn’t need to complain, or, so the assumption he made of himself.

The gravel-stricken road he stood upon grinded under his shoes as he shuffled forth a little to examine the other angle of the house. Since nothing was outwardly compelling to note, he swung his head to face down the path they’d rode up.\
It appeared as if the road led back to the inner dwellings of the unknown.. Or rather, the thicket of trees beyond that comprised a forest, shrouded in shadow from the canopies above.\
\
“Seems we shaved off a rather ample amount of time,” Elijah mumbled once he returned to her side, gazing at her copper-plated pocket watch, which she re-stuffed back into her skirt.\
“We’ll only be a moment!” She then called, hoisting her luggage with renewed sense of jubilation to the coachman, who agreed with some noise.                                                                                                           

Zane wasn’t able to adjust further before the woman hustled him toward the house and up the few steps, his heels digging into the ply-wood underneath as he was effortlessly shuffled to the entryway.\
\
“Elijah!” He exclaimed in an exasperated manner, nearly tripping over his soles as his nose just about whacked right into glass molded into the door.

“Oh, Sir Zane, I was simply escorting you—”\
“*You,*” he punctuated with a whirl of his heel, prodding his finger straight into her collarbone, “of all people, are not allowed to *man-handle* me to such an *egregiously disrespectful* extent.” He retorted, testing the handle once he let go of his luggage and shuddering the door open with one last contemptuous look in her direction. The lack of keys hidden under a mat or flowerpot didn’t so much as irk him as she just had, storing the thought for later.

Unfolded before them, honestly, was quite little of a sight at first.

The dim vestibule had been clipped short, its end shrouded in darkness, while near the door allowed for a space to discard one's footwear and a closet beside the entryway.\
Elijah almost tilted forward, maneuvering her skirt to work herself free of her tan, ankle-high boots before it dawned upon her that—why, that they were on a somewhat tight schedule, now, weren’t they?

Zane tried not to make an effort to glance much at the house yet, however still he noticed glimmers of embers flickered on the surface of a sleek chest of drawers in the middle of a walk-way, candles snug in bronze with glass cupping the ornaments’ base. A series of pine splinters hugged the globe of glass, dotted with red berries of various hues. Beside the stand ticked a grandfather clock nigh noiselessly, its pendulum causing quite the ruckus in conjunction.

“Why… it appears rather *cozy* I’d say,” began Elijah, hopeful to raise the mood, maybe to even distract Zane and coax him into lingering a bit longer (despite her earlier comment to the coachman)—only to get shouldered by Zane in sheer, abrupt crudeness.\
He, being a busy man, had little time for semantics.                                                   

Dwindling about rather than heading over to plunge his wrath upon the perpetrator who so graciously seized his property was one of the fewer things on the list of his agenda for the remainder of the day.

The coachman side-eyed Zane's return, peering back at how quickly he bustled onto the carriage, acutely raising his brows as Elijah awkwardly giggled to cover what little disappointment she felt in pursuit. Hurriedly, she closed the door to avoid any confrontation (was there any to be had) and settled unpleasantly back into her seat, which retained little warmth.

It was only a moment before the carriage jerked into motion and steadily picked up momentum, swaying the two back and fourth as the coachman took an elaborate berth that took quite some doing, and back they tunneled through their earlier path. Well, *supposedly*, of course, since Zane had been unconscious for the duration of the ride over.

A strong bustle set Zane’s fingers to find their way into the cushion underneath him, digging at the seat to steady himself.

When no noise presented itself from either coachman nor Elijah—save for the birdsong in the treetops, rather—he turned inwardly to his own slate of churning thoughts that demanded sorting.

*What chance do I have that the authorities might be of use?* He lingered over sentence, marinating on the scale of use people in power usually are. 

Funneling further thought into it, he promptly shook his head at the image of those pesky law enforcement individuals disregarding his request, glossing it over with a presently far accurate scenario in his mind. *Unlikely. Those fools remain incredibly lazy lest there’s some sort of compensation in it for them. Not that I’ve planned to pay a pair of pigs for something such as this.*

“May I inquire you both on where the next stop should be?” The coachman hollered, and Elijah jumped, scrambling to roll down the window—operated by a handheld crank—to answer him in kind.

While she blurted her description on the closest thing they would search for, Zane’s eyes wandered down to her breast. There, encapsulating the shape of her chest, was the strap of her leather bag, partially concealing his fathers’ family and company crest embroidered on her maroon jacket.

Circular and nearly a badge, almost, embedded with curved diamond-shaped stars all along the ridge. A smaller circle within the overall design had a black pen, adorned at the top of the nib were curved branches that stretched out, their ends something that of weight scales—All to follow the overall design of judgement. 

“Why, as luck would have it, there happens to be a company that fits your description, young lady!”

Zane flinched at the sheer volume of which the declaration was bellowed. A crease in his lip dug deeper with irritation.

“Why, that’s excellent… !” She turned her attention to him from the window, “Did you hear, Zane?” She leaned in close, the scrunch in her shoulders suggesting far more interesting news, perhaps as if they were invited to the gala, rather than… well, on a manhunt for information regarding a specific individual.

“I’m not quite deaf ye–”

“Ashoft’s the name,” called the coachman, before Zane could finish—to the dirty-blonde’s credit, he’d managed to restrain his threads from going out and silencing the man with methods that would cripple the poor soul for the rest of his days.

*Behave,* he scolded his threads with a disdainful scrunch of his nose, hiking up his shirt collar and ignoring their current incessant swarming in his mind, which.. was no task to sneeze at. 

It was as if he were trying to hold a bunch of hounding rottweilers on a leash from a backyard squirrel, *such unreasonable things, these strings.*

Yet… he couldn’t always *blame* them in situations like these. They… they only desired to be *used*. Given the slightest reason, they were happy to oblige in dealing with the instigator of his issues swiftly.

They were, in a sense, limbs. Spindly limbs, that is, that consisted of unknown material. (However…  If Zane had to put it into perspective for the common folk to understand… It would be the equivalent of tying a man's arm behind his back for a few years, sparingly letting him use it to stretch for a few moments at a time.)

“—And… I assume there will be a line… So, if it wouldn’t be too much trouble for you, perhaps I can wander around town… a-and…” Zane tuned Elijah back in, watching her hand flip in vague gestures as she collected words to muster up another sentence, “... Fetch us some groceries. While you… while you wait.”

Zane proceeded to analyze Elijah surreptitiously through the navy tinted lenses of his shades. He unapologetically disguised the fact he had his mind settling vastly important matters towards a solution, however he felt confident he had enough information to continue the discussion.\
“And.. perhaps a nice restaurant for lunch?” She offered sheepishly under his gaze, sinking into her shoulders.

“...Fine,” he relinquished, but the word alone took some… doing. Aigh.. to comply with just about *anyone*..\
So maybe it irked him a touch. He wouldn’t admit *that* (*food however.. Sounded splendid. So.. truly little to complain over*).                                                                                     

“That’s fine.”

“Consider it done.” She piped up with a small smile, gleeful to be of some use.

The rest of the trip hadn’t lasted long, especially when Zane diverted his attention towards further inner reflection, sorting through phrases to bring to…\
Oh. He.. he hadn’t quite devoted much thought to anything else up until this very moment—but who *would* he converse with? He’d never undergone this sort of process. Holding his own in a conversation was no problem, but.. Semantics of professionalism with members of the populous rather than a trained instructor…

*If systems were set, they’d have to be established in such fashion of simple use.* He convinced himself whilst scratching at one of his canines absently, his carriage rattling and separating his temple from the cold glass and bump against the surface, eye wincing to the contact. 

*Right*?

After another series of gazing at multiple architecturally exquisite and sound buildings of Victorian styling and some odd conversation Zane caught right as soon as he rolled down the window to release some of the stuffy air— “—And the fellow insisted I sell the vacuum cleaner—and why, Margret? Why? Well, it was just gathering dust!”

In a strikingly close perimeter to the first voice, followed:

“Gerald, you butter-bellied hog! Quit your instance of telling those sardine-lined puns to our customers!”

“Would you like to hear a joke about paper, Margret?”

“No!”

“Oh, that's plenty fine. It's *tear-able* regardless.”

“***GERALD***!”

Zane, despite seeing the passerby with a subtle grin they withheld over their expressions, some beckoning his friend to not-quite-whisper into another's ear: “\*It’s the Morst Twins again.. Always those two bickering, aren’t they\*”... withdrew his head from the carriage window after catching wind of the hubub.\
“I think that’s… er—enough *fresh air* for now.” He conceded, gingerly screwing the handle to snuff out the outside world.

All too soon the carriage shuddered to a halt as they’d rolled to a part in the sidewalk, the coachman guiding the horses beside the curb neatly.\
“We’re here!” Was the unnecessary shout from the coachman, as Zane (with a scoff) had already scooted off to the carriage door, easing it open with a shoulder, hiking up his shirt collar around his neck.

\
*Ought to get it over and done with.*

\
“You know where I’ll be.” Zane told Elijah flatly, casting her a glance to ensure she was listening, skipping a step and hopping straight to the curb, and slamming the door.\
While unbeknownst to him, it earned him a touch of a glare from the coachmen, he remained unbothered as his gaze wandered upon the paved stone steps of a finely crafted establishment…

“ *‘Ashoft… Public Records Building…’* ” Confirming it was the place, he ducked his head to the carved stone staircase beyond. 

“I don’t suppose there’s a better place suitable.” Murmured Zane under his breath, glancing this way and that to ensure he wouldn’t wander into a man or two.\
As he trudged up the steps, he etched the columns of the place in mind—sturdy, he’ll give the artisans that. They began tapering near the end, the more profound base of keeping the roof aloft.\
In all honesty, it more resembled a town hall rather than an archive containing records.

“Swell suit you’ve got there,” came a man down the steps, tipping his hat. 

Zane nodded curtly.\
“Thank you.” He managed, while he didn’t so much as offer the stranger a glance, coming to a stone platform where potted, whilst browning, lengthy shrubbery framed the two doors of the establishment.

*Looks as though business is flourishing—wouldn’t be able to afford this otherwise, could they? Or perhaps they funneled funds from the town..* He pinned the bends of his shirt collar between his fingers, fully concealing his threads from view.\
\

“Ah–” Zane found his fingers tightly capturing the painted metal handle beside the staircase with vigor.

With a hiss, he managed a: “*Quiet, you*,” as he palmed his temple, ruffling what bangs rested over the bridge of his nose with his fingers.\
“*Aigh*…” He sighed.\
Try as he might, coaxing the voices buzzing noisily in his skull to a dull, aching whisper wasn’t quite in their best interest, and disregarded him effortlessly.

*When will my efforts prevail..* He thought irritatedly, stalking towards the handle of the door and tugging towards his body with intent on being quick about the ordeal.\
Unlike the train door this one had stable, lubricated hinges, and didn’t creak noisily.

\
*Though, it was a sliding door, wasn’t it?* Occurred to Zane, *Nevermind.*

Dim light met him, and luckily, he had no need to adjust. Immediately following he met with a lobby a short distance from the hallway littered with boards of cork with newspaper plastered all along the surface, stamped securely with nails all across the pages: corners, the middle of the some with a dozen or one.\
\
There, at the lobby, were several chairs lined along the walls, a collective strip or more seats settled a few paces in front of him. While he wasn’t sure what he’d been expecting, he at least assumed as much as the space loaded with customers.

He then adjusted his concentration to the further reaches of the room and noticed the reception desk. Or desks. No, it was certainly a singular desk, rounded evenly.\
Each segment was separated in a third by a wall, you see, and separate serving windows, which had been currently managed by a singular employee at the moment. And…\
*I give you my thanks, lady luck.* Thought Zane sarcastically, frowning at the occupancy at the first window. The man spoke in a hushed voice to whoever was serving him, and Zane couldn’t take a peek. Though it would serve him no purpose regardless.

\
Uneasily he smoothed his ruffled vest after the continuously lengthy pause. Should he… sit? Waiting was a given… Perhaps… he could ask a representative somewhere else in the building?\
“Next!” Bellowed—\
Zane’s visage contorted. A *woman*. British, a voice compellingly deeper a few octaves, but unmistakable female, and certainly sultry. But—by the suns. He’s only heard the worst of rumors with the females in the workforce.\
Okay, he eased himself, some twisted glee settling in his stomach to the thought of dispatching her.

“Next in line!”

Zane jerked so harshly to the noise he’d let one of his threads loosen and lash outward. Hastily he grabbed it and strangled it down, grappling it back under his shirt with a hiss—The sun was thankfully beaming a portion of its radiance against his back, offering astounding amounts of cover for his ministrations.\
“Next!” The woman impatiently called again.\
“One moment-!” He stuffed the aggravated thread into his undershirt and felt it mournfully sag into the folds of the fabric, gingerly pulling himself out of the seat cushion that normally would have sunk under his weight if the material hadn’t been so *stiff*. And unremorsefully *itchy*.\
Somehow he managed to arrive to the desk before any other man managed, pinning his shirt collar instinctively as he drove himself to a halt.\
Now, Zane on impulse desired to usher a polite ‘sorry’ for his behavior, but every remaining spec of logic (as he convinced himself) stamped it out immediately.\
\

Her mascara pegged her as imposing, though her exhausted expression suggested less of that impression. Tucked between her index and middle fingers was a smoldering fag—she held it away from a likely expensive gown of marigold yellow, a splattering of flowers matched with frills; along the frills brush lay a bold embossing tag displaying the name:

*Uncouth for a woman, much?* He eyed the cigar with particular distaste, wrinkling his nose to the putrid scent wafting around her. Were you to inspect her any closer and you could practically make the outline of the smog.\
And oh, it clung like *muck*, and were Zane a betting man, he’d certainly say he’d gamble a few of his gold coins to the possibility he’d be smelling like her for a few hours at most.                               

*Possibly ruins the quality of those pearls practically raining down those breast of hers… what a skag.* He’d added, as if his already sour impression of her wasn’t enough.\
Zane admitted reluctantly of how thankful he’d been that no-one had some wire to his thoughts.

Back to this sorry excuse of a woman… Don’t start him on her visage, whether carved into her was due to concentration from hours on end or loathing for her current predicament in the work force, it was difficult to differentiate. Perhaps she could have been a woman of class… though with this attitude she holstered like some kind of prize, he would have had trouble believing.

\
“I require information on a specific individual.” He paused, weighing the options of giving her more insight, or if that was all he needed. He decided on the former, “one of who has possession of one of my… possessions.”

\
*This shouldn't even be a problem I have to solve..*

\
“Stolen?” She asked… or corrected. Her tone carried little hint at her angle.

She raised her brow rather than her eyes, as if the act alone was enough to satisfy him—all while she ducked her body into that of a strange shape as Zane soon realized she was rummaging for something in a cabinet beside her.\
“That it would be, yes.”\
“Name?”

*Would she be able to actually grant me the information I’d need?* He wondered. *This could be a relative waste of time. Though if it were all for naught then why would this place be standing, still?*

It occurred to him after a brief moment that the woman was inspecting him with speculation. He had to reflect on what she’d asked a mere moment ago, and answering with the nature of a jutting start to an engine: “Ah, er… Zane. Zane Wolfgang.” His lack of attention in such a demanding situation was that of hilarity.\
\
She sounded the name out between the cigar as she jotted his name down upon an appeal with a pencil.. Or a… form, of sorts? He cocked his head to attempt to decipher the upside-down lettering, but remained unsuccessful apart from watching her scribble the year *1925* and his name. He raised his head as she asked his birthday, and he off-handedly responded automatically: *Third o’ January, nineteen o’ five.*.

The place behind her was bigger than he’d give credit for, really, the longer he allowed himself a look. Metal cabinets side by side, one or two open with nooks of files clambering over the top, notes upon select few he could almost make the words of had he squinted.

A few wooden desks amassed much of the space along with spindly chairs and potted plants of the long-leaved variety, though retained the sort of atmosphere one would snag from a book; studiously produced by an author who hadn’t strayed far from his isolated studio with the strict imagination of a board of plywood.

It accentuated a kind of blandness that hadn’t been often revealed to the naked eye, even with breaths of paintings slabbed along the dull gold walls. 

\
*Well.. At least some of the desks appeared personalized with belongings.* 

However.. He’d always labeled those exaggerations as hogwash when he encountered those miserable novels, but.. he supposed reality was the best reference, however secluded those authors were.

“You said.. identification … of… an *individuaal*.. “ He caught more of what she uttered, and allowed his eyes to flit back to the paper her fingers kissed the edges of. She’d shown him a tilt of her chin as her eyes scanned the parchment she inscribed upon over the lip of her rectangular glasses.

“I’ll need a general description.” She stated, matter–of–factly, then promptly lifted her head with a snap of her neck so suddenly, Zane prepared himself to witness her self-decapitation.\
“Oh, er, she–”\
“CLEMETINE!”\
Zane jerked his upper-half so harshly his lower-half was unable to keep pace, keeping him rooted to the spot.

“*CLEMETINE*!” She bellowed again with that same impatience, Zane tilting his head and covering an ear with a palm. No voice of that octave should have come from a measly woman. Perhaps this was the work of the workforce of day to days…

“Y-yes! Yes Miss, yes, yes… you require my services?” A small voice, eclipsed by Laurice tenfold.\
“Listen well to this man. He was victim of a robbery, so the criminal must be of our locals.. Would be a madman to stray far from his home.. Give the details, give the details now..” She rambled on and on as she wrote more on the form.

“Oh my stars. Why, haven’t you gone to the police?” Zane finally perked his head up to witness a girl with buttery-gold-and-brown locks, and a soft face much like Elijah’s, without the European spin.\
She wore mute gray frills to match her eyes, as if she desired nothing more than to become the color of the ground outside than to meddle with folks and do her job.\

“This woman, this one here? You’re one of the better men I’ve encountered, and I’ll tell you what—wise up, and wise up quick and leave this town before you become another bloody head that’ll roll out on the street for those lot.”\
“Excuse—?”\
“That woman is nothing but trouble!” She hollered. “You forget her face, you forget this town. You forget whatever that is you lost and get that ass of yours on a train this *instance*!”

\*What a shrill, indignant squawk this woman has!\* He thought incredulously, snarling, “I’ve come here after my precious hours spent, and *you ask me to leave-!?*”

“I’m in no position to tell you what you do with your life, but LISTEN WELL.” She cried over him, tossing her arms in the air. Has you been in another room you could have mistaken her for a parrot by the way she spoke!

“Just this week two thick oaf aristocrats waltzed in here demanding we identify some robber. Two, you hear? *Again and again* they come with the same two descriptions—a woman and man. The man is a nigger, downright vile man I tell you, downright vile! Oh, and what a swell sight to see when those damned posters come up and show them men missing!” She wheezed out of breath, deliriously blind by anger from Zane’s oblivousness. “Do you think I *enjoy* sitting here, week after week, meeting clients whose heads roll the day after the next despite my warnings? Do you!?” She was about to blow her top off from the steam rising off her face, grilling the stricken man before her. “You’d be mad to pursue her, mad!”\
He hardly could even comprehend the mere fact she’d risen her face against him; that alone would have warranted his hand to fly on his own (had there been no glass barrier), but her logic had rattled him furthermore.\
“Swallow that pride of yours, son.” She landed back in her seat, out of fumes. “Tuck the tail between your legs n’ go. Keep that head of yours. Any one object can be replaced, but not a life.”



 **                                                           . —«——⁜——»— .**



Zane held the copy of a document of this *Rachel* girl’s information a few minutes later, finding himself seated on one of the first few stone steps toward Ashoft’s Public Records Archive, or what other name it went by.\
He felt as defused as the woman had, and defeated as his threads felt among the rustles of his clothes. As though the clouds felt this, they accentuated the gloomy atmosphere by swallowing up the sun as a great mythical beast would.

\
*So she doesn’t have a labeled residency, or else she’d have been dealt with.* He ticked off any sort of searching through occupation, as well. Information about her was things he relatively knew, and everything was null and void.

\
*Her birthday, however.. It feels as though it could be some use but tracing that all would make for some mighty work.* He sighed, leaning further onto his cheek in distress.\
As much as it tormented him to admit, the woman was right. Rings were often passed down or they crafted new ones depending on the size of the wearer… though he couldn’t quite admit to his father not only he’d snuck from his quarters outside the walls for the genuine first time of his life, that would be a difficult thing to articulate.

*Hello, father. My deepest condolences, but upon my first escapade into the real world, none by your permission, I’ve happened to have lost the account of my most precious possession that could easily account for fraud in our family had the ring fallen into the hands of folk wise enough to recognize the power she wields. Surely by chance you could provide me another one despite going behind your back for this whole tribulation?*

*\- Sincerely, your least adored son,*\
*Zane Wolfgang*

Yes, an excellent idea.\
Zane’s mouth formed into a thin line.\
“I think it would be far more productive if I just found a sharp stone and bashed my head against it.” He spoke to nobody in particular as he laid himself across a few of the steps, rolling his scalp against the grinding of the stone and eyeing the clouds above.

“That would rather be an appalling idea, let alone a sight, Sir Zane.”\
Zane, with scrutiny, angled down his eyes to where the demure shape of Elijah materialized, carrying a wicker basket full of what he assumed groceries—he couldn’t see from the combined power of the rims of his glasses to the basket's angle.

“I think not.” He rolled off of his back to hunch over his thighs with his forearms. **..Oh, what a day…*.*

“Are you ready for lunch?” Inquired the voice above him now, as he accepted his place here to rot for the remainder of eternity. “I hear they’ve a bevy of restaurants to pick from.”\
\
*So terribly a foolish idea of mine not so much as follow her path*… The contingently depressive voice spoke in his skull as he stood fully, shoulders a-sagging.\
“I suppose it wouldn’t be wise to deplete my energy just yet.” He conceded, following her to the sidewalk, where the carriage yawned it’s door open in welcome.
